Understanding The Pace Of Time


Understanding the difference between chronos and kairos does not immediately resolve the difficulty of waiting in a season that has not yet produced what you are hoping for. But it does something more important: it reframes the waiting. Chronos time spent in a season of preparation is not wasted time. It is the necessary substrate for the kairos moment that the preparation is building toward. The seed does not waste its winter. The winter is the specific, irreplaceable condition under which the biochemical processes of germination are completed. Without the winter, there is no spring. Without the chronos of preparation, there is no kairos of harvest.
