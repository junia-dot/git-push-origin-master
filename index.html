Understanding the Pace of Time

You have been living with a particular understanding of time for as long as you can remember.
It arrived before you were old enough to question it. It was delivered by clocks on classroom walls, by schedules handed to you on the first day of school, by the relentless ticking of a world that measured progress in minutes, quarters, semesters, and fiscal years. By the time you were old enough to have opinions about how your life was going, the clock was already the primary instrument by which you judged it.

Purpose Over Pleasure VOL 1

At some point, every person arrives at a crossroads. It does not always look dramatic. Sometimes it is a quiet moment — a job offer, a relationship, a habit forming in the dark, a dream you keep postponing. Sometimes it is loud — a crisis that strips away every comfortable option and forces a decision you can no longer avoid. But in every form it takes, the crossroads asks the same question: what will you choose? This book is about that question. Specifically, it is about the choice between purpose — the God-authored design for your life — and pleasure — the immediate, the comfortable, and the self-directed

VOL 2

Volume Two is structured around three essential questions. First: how do you identify when pleasure is working against your purpose? Second: what are the specific mechanisms — the seven keys — through which pleasure operates on purpose? And third: what does it actually look like, in practical terms, to bring pleasure under control? These are not abstract theological questions. They are the questions of a person who has a calling and is serious about protecting it. They are the questions of someone who has noticed a pattern in their own life and wants to understand it before it costs them something they cannot recover...

VOL 3

If Volume 1 gave you a map and Volume 2 warned you of the dangers along the road, Volume 3 is the moment you plant your feet and decide to walk. This final section was written with a deep sense of responsibility. Because by the time a reader arrives here, they have already encountered the weight of what purpose requires and the cost of what pleasure takes. The question is no longer about understanding — it is about decision. And decisions of this magnitude deserve to be supported, not just challenged. Volume Three exists to do that supporting work. It presents the benefits — all twenty-eight of them — not as incentives to manipulate a choice, but as honest evidence of what a purpose-driven life actually produces over time. It offers the biblical framework that holds it all together...
